# 📊 IBM HR Workforce Attrition Analysis

**Domain:** HR Analytics + Data Analytics + Business Intelligence + Workforce Analytics

---

# 1. Problem Statement

Employee attrition is one of the major challenges faced by organizations, resulting in increased recruitment costs, reduced productivity, and loss of experienced employees. HR departments need data-driven insights to understand employee behavior and identify the factors contributing to workforce attrition.

This project analyzes the IBM HR Analytics dataset using Python and Power BI to identify attrition patterns, evaluate workforce health, and provide actionable recommendations to improve employee retention.

---

# 2. Dataset Description

- **Dataset:** IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset
- **Total Employees:** 1,470
- **Total Features:** 50 Columns
- **Source:** IBM HR Analytics Dataset
- **Format:** CSV

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| Age | Employee age |
| Gender | Employee gender |
| Department | Employee department |
| JobRole | Employee designation |
| MonthlyIncome | Monthly salary |
| TotalWorkingYears | Employee experience |
| WorkLifeBalance | Work-life balance score |
| JobSatisfaction | Job satisfaction rating |
| OverTime | Overtime status |
| Attrition | Employee attrition (Yes/No) |

# 5. Key Findings

- Sales department recorded the highest employee attrition.
- Employees in the Low Salary Band experienced higher attrition.
- Employees working overtime showed a greater likelihood of leaving the organization.
- Poor Work-Life Balance was strongly associated with employee attrition.
- Lower Job Satisfaction significantly increased employee turnover.
- Research & Development had the highest employee population.
- Employees with fewer years of service were more likely to leave.
- High-Risk Employees require immediate HR intervention and retention strategies.

---

# 6. Recommendations

- Improve employee work-life balance through flexible work policies.
- Reduce excessive overtime to minimize employee burnout.
- Review compensation for employees in lower salary bands.
- Strengthen employee engagement and recognition programs.
- Provide career development and training opportunities.
- Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys.
- Implement targeted retention programs for high-risk employees.
- Focus HR initiatives on departments with higher attrition rates.
